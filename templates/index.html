The Nutritional Landscape of Recipes
Fig 02

PCA map of recipe nutrition profiles, where each point is a recipe positioned by its overall nutritional composition and colored by nutrition cluster.

The Nutritional Landscape
Dimensionality Reduction · Recipe Nutrition Space
What this shows

Each point represents a recipe. Its position is determined by a PCA projection of nutritional variables, so recipes with similar overall nutrient composition appear close together. Colors indicate automatically detected nutrition clusters rather than manually assigned food categories.

How to read it

Distances in the plot reflect nutritional similarity: nearby recipes tend to share similar balances of calories, protein, sugar, sodium, fat, and carbohydrate-related features. The horizontal and vertical axes are latent nutrition dimensions extracted from the full feature space.

Why it matters

This moves the analysis beyond ingredient structure alone. While the ingredient network shows which ingredients appear together, this figure shows how entire recipes organize themselves in nutrition space, revealing groups of recipes that may look different categorically but behave similarly nutritionally.
